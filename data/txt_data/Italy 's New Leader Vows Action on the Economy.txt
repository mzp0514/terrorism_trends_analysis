Italy's New Leader Vows Action on the Economy

Byline: Special to the New York Times
Dateline: ROME, Oct. 22
Body


Italy's new Prime Minister, Arnaldo Forlani, presented his four-party coalition Government to Parliament today with a policy declaration in which he stressed his awareness of the country's growing economic difficulties and promised to wage a more effective fight against inflation and unemployment.
Mr. Forlani, a former Foreign Minister, pledged continuity of Italy's strong support for the North Atlantic Treaty Organization and of its friendship with the United States.
Italy's new Prime Minister, Arnaldo Forlani, presents his coalition's policies to Parliament; stresses effective economic actionHe called for ratification by the American Senate of the strategic arms limitation treaty between the United States and the Soviet Union.
Mr. Forlani, the head of the dominant Christian Democratic Party, vowed to press the Italian Government's campaign against terrorism, kidnappings and the Mafia.
In an indirect appeal to the Communists to give his Government a chance, he asked the opposition parties, without naming them, to be constructive in their attitude and promised to consult with them.  The Communists, the country's second largest party, had been conducting a systematic campaign against Mr. Forlani's predecessor, Francesco Cossiga.
