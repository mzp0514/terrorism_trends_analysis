1639 New College was renamed Harvard College for clergyman John Harvard.
1925 The Tennessee General Assembly approved a bill prohibiting the teaching of the theory of evolution.
1955 Henry Kaufmann, 94, pioneer Pittsburgh merchant and one of the four founders of Kaufmann's department stores, died in New York City.
1964 Bar manager Catherine "Kitty" Genovese, 28, was stabbed to death near her Queens, N.Y. home; the case generated controversy over the supposed failure of Genovese's neighbors to respond to her cries for help.
1980 Ford Motor Chairman Henry Ford II announced he was stepping down, on the same day that a jury in Winamac, Ind., found the company not guilty of reckless homicide in the fiery deaths of three young women in a Ford Pinto.
2000 Pittsburgh native Dan Marino retired from football after a long Hall of Fame career with the Miami Dolphins.
2002 President George W. Bush declared at a news conference that Iraqi President Saddam Hussein was a menace "and we're going to deal with him," and said Osama bin Laden had been reduced to a marginal figure in the war on terrorism.
Today's birthdays: Jazz musician Roy Haynes, 87. Singer-songwriter Neil Sedaka, 73. Actor William H. Macy, 62. Actress Dana Delany, 56. Rock musician Adam Clayton (U2), 52. Rapper-actor Common, 40. Actor Emile Hirsch, 27.
Thought for today: "If you lose the power to laugh, you lose the power to think."
-- Clarence Darrow, American lawyer (born 1857, died this date in 1938)
