A remembrance ceremony adding the names of two North Dakota National Guard soldiers killed last year in Afghanistan to a memorial will take place at 6 p.m. today at the Memorial to the Fallen in the Global War on Terrorism.
Sgt. 1st Class Darren Linde of Devils Lake and Spc. Tyler Orgaard of Bismarck were killed by a roadside bomb while on a mission.
Both were members of the North Dakota National Guard's 818th Engineer Company (Sapper) and died on Dec. 3 during combat operations in Lashkar Gah, Helmand Province, Afghanistan.
Linde, 41, formerly of Sidney, Mont., is interred at Sidney Cemetery, Sidney, Mont. Orgaard, is interred at North Dakota Veterans Cemetery, Mandan.
The memorial is a joint endeavor between the city of Bismarck and the North Dakota National Guard and is located outside the main entrance at Fraine Barracks.
It was dedicated on Sept. 11, 2009, and honors North Dakota service members who were killed during the global war on terrorism.
In case of bad weather, the ceremony will be moved indoors to build-ing 400 at Fraine Barracks.
Connie Sprynczynatyk, civilian aide to the secretary of the Army, will serve as the ceremony's emcee.
The keynote speaker is Harriet Goodiron. Others scheduled to speak include Gov. Jack Dalrymple, Rep. Kevin Cramer, R-N.D., and Maj. Gen. David Sprynczynatyk, North Dakota adjutant general.
The event is open to the public and parking is available at the Bank of North Dakota.
- Brian Gehring
