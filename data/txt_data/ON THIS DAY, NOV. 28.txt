1520: Portuguese navigator Ferdinand Magellan reached the Pacific Ocean after passing through the South American strait that now bears his name.
1881: Pittsburgh Catholic College of the Holy Ghost, now Duquesne University, received a state charter.
1909: Sergei Rachmaninoff's Piano Concerto No. 3 in D minor, Op. 30, had its world premiere in New York, with Rachmaninoff at the piano.
1947: The Pittsburgh Board of Education adopted an $18 million budget, an increase of $3 million over 1947, and levied a new $5-a-year head tax on all city residents over 21 years old.
2017: Ahmed Abu Khattala, a Libyan militant, was convicted of terrorism charges in federal court in Washington stemming from the 2012 Benghazi attacks that killed the U.S. ambassador to Libya and three other Americans.
