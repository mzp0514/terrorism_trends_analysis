Frank Amoroso, the regional director for the U. S. Department of Justice's Boston bureau will speak at the 2 p.m. Saturday meeting of the Maine Chapter of the Association of Former Intelligence Officers at the Kennebunk Free Public Library.
Amoroso's talk will detail the negative effects of terrorism on Muslims and non-Muslims alike.
Amoroso is involved with the Community Relations Service, the Justice Department's "peacemaker" for community conflicts and tensions arising from differences of race, color and national origin. Created by the Civil Rights Act of 1964, CRS is a federal agency dedicated to assist state and local units of government, private and public organizations and community groups by preventing and resolving racial and ethnic tensions, incidents and civil disorders, as well as in restoring racial stability and harmony. The CRS deploys skilled professional conciliators to assist individuals of diverse racial and cultural backgrounds.
Amoroso holds a bachelor's degree in sociology from the University of Maine and a master's degree in counseling from Hampton University. He is a graduate of the Maine Executive Institute, Maine Maritime Academy.
The sessions are open to the public.
The library is at 112 Main St.
For more details, call AFIO President Barbara Storer at 985-2392.
