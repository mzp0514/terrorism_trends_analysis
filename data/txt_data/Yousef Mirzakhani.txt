Body


Yousef Nafchi Mirzakhani, 32, Mandan, formerly of Charleston, W.Va., passed away on Nov. 23, 2013. Services were held on Dec. 2 in Charleston.
Yousef was an automated logistics specialist in the Army National Guard with over 10 years active duty service in the Army. It was during those 10 years of active duty that he served two deployments in the Iraq war. Yousef served his country honorably as a staff sergeant in the U.S. Army and was decorated with the Army Commendation Medal with three clusters, Army Achievement with two clusters, Army Good Conduct Medal with two clusters, the National Defense Medal, Iraqi Campaign Medal with two Campaign Stars, Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal and Service Medal, the NDOPD Ribbon with two clusters, Army Service Ribbon, Overseas Service Ribbon, Combat Action Badge and the Driver/Mechanic Badge (Driver Clasp).
He is survived by his wife, Amy Mirzakhani and their daughter, Veeda, both of Mandan; his parents, Bayazali Mirzakhani and Ehteram Kazemi; his brothers, Fazi, Ramin (Dawn), Sadegh (Najmeh), Amad (Cassandra) and Ahmad Mirzakhani, all of Charleston; his sisters, Mina (Hamid), Huntington, W. Va., Sarah Mirzakhani, Falls Church, Va., and Sheeva Mirzakhani, Charleston; two nieces, Azyn and Sheridan; and three nephews, Kamron, Maksim and Armeen. (Eastgate Funeral Service, Bismarck)
