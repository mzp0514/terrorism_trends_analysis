(RTTNews) - An accused al-Qaeda operative working within the United States has pleaded guilty to federal conspiracy charges.
The U.S. Attorney&apos;s office said 43-year-old Ali Saleh Kahlah al-Marri entered the plea Thursday to a charge of conspiracy to provide material support to al-Qaeda.
In a news release, Attorney General Eric Holder said al-Marri admitted that he agreed with others "to provide material support or resources to al-Qaeda in the form of personnel, including himself, to work under al-Qaeda&apos;s direction and control with the intent to further the terrorist activity of terrorism objectives of al-Qaeda.
The plea was issued in U.S. District Court in Illinois, where Judge Michael Mihm set sentencing for July 30. Al-Marri faces a prison term of up to 15 years, a $250,000 fine and other penalties.
"Ali al-Marri was an al-Qaeda &apos;sleeper&apos; operative working on U.S. soil and directed by the chief planner of the 9-11 attacks," said FBI National Security Branch official Arthur Cummings.. "Al-Marri researched the use of chemical weapons, potential targets and maximum casualties."
"Ali al-Marri today admitted that he traveled to central Illinois as an al-Qaeda operative the day before the Sept.11, 2001, attacks to plan and prepare for future acts of terrorism within the United States," said Jeffrey B. Lang, Acting U.S. Attorney for the Central District of Illinois.
