President Obama leaves Friday on a nine-day trip to India, Indonesia, South Korea and Japan for talks on the global economy, trade and terrorism.
Here is his schedule so far:
1.
Nov. 6-7: Mumbai, India
*Speaks about terrorism from the Taj hotel, which was torched during the 2008 terrorist attacks; visits the Gandhi Museum; speaks to U.S.-India Business Council meeting.
*Attends school celebration of Diwali holiday; holds town hall with university students.
2.
Nov. 7-8: New Delhi
*Visits Humaya's tomb; attends private dinner with Prime Minister Manmohan Singh and his wife.
*Lays wreath on Mohandas Gandhi's grave; meets with Singh and holds joint news conference; addresses Indian Parliament; attends state dinner in his honor.
3.
Nov. 9-10: Jakarta, Indonesia
*Meets with President Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono and holds joint news conference; attends official dinner in his honor.
*Lays wreath at Heroes Cemetery; visits Istiqlal Mosque; delivers speech to the Indonesian people.
4.
Nov. 10-11: Seoul
*Marks Veterans Day by speaking to U.S. troops at Yongsan Garrison; meets with President Lee Myung Bak and holds joint news conference; meets with President Hu Jintao of China; attends G-20 working dinner.
*Attends G-20 meetings and lunch; holds press conference.
5.
Nov. 12-14: Yokohama, Japan
*Speaks to CEO business summit; meets with Prime Minister Naoto Kan of Japan; meets with Prime Minister Julia Gillard of Australia; attends APEC meetings, including APEC lunch and dinner.
*Meets with Prime Minister Dmitry Medvedev of Russia; attends APEC lunch; visits the city's great Buddha statue; returns to Washington.
Sources: USA TODAY research and the White House
