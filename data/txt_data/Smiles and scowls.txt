Smiles and scowls
Charleston Gazette-Mail
June 18, 2016, Saturday


Copyright 2016 Charleston Newspapers
Section: EDITORIAL; Pg. P4A
Length: 218 words
Byline: Gzedit
Body


To more than 100 gifted teens from around America and the world who assembled in Charleston for the yearly National Youth Science Camp in the mountains.
To Monsignor Edward Sadie, a pillar of midtown Charleston, retiring from Sacred Heart Co-Cathedral - where an outdoor gathering spot has been named "Sadie Space in his honor.
To Sen. Joe Manchin, D-W.Va., who joined a filibuster to prevent gun sales to suspects on terrorism watch lists.
To Sen. Shelley Capito, R-W.Va., who previously voted to allow gun sales to suspects on the terrorism watch list.
To Dominion Resources, which donated $15,000 to Friends of Blackwater to upgrade mountain trails in Tucker County.
To Sarah Kessick of Hansford, who subdued a burglar and held him until police arrived.
To Alpha Technology of South Charleston, which wants to invest $5 million in a 30-mile broadband loop that would allow growth of high-tech businesses in the mid-Kanawha Valley
To Walmart, which donated $118,000 to Catholic Charities West Virginia for food programs across the state.
To IBEX Global Solutions, which plans to add 60 to 70 more telecommunications jobs at Elkins, where the firm already employs 90.
To Treasurer John Perdue, who returned a record-breaking $12.2 million of unclaimed property to West Virginians in the fiscal year that ends June 30.
