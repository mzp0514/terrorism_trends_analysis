CALAIS -- "The Tiger's Wife" by Tea Obreht is the book for Calais Bookshop's Wednesday, Nov. 30, reading group. This is the 68th book group in an ongoing reading program available at Calais Bookshop, 405 Main St. Group begins at 5:30 p.m. New participants welcome. For schedules and information, call 454-1110.
CAMDEN -- Book talk by Curt Stager, paleoecologist and author of "Deep Future: The Next 100,000 Years of Life on Earth," 3:30 p.m. Friday, Nov. 25, at Camden Public Library, 55 Main St. For information, call 236-3440.
CAMDEN -- Tom McCarthy, former Secret Service agent, will present a talk on terrorism and his experience in training foreign governments and domestic agencies in anti-terrorism, 6:30 p.m. Tuesday, Nov. 29, at Camden Public Library, 55 Main St. For information, call 236-3440.
PORTLAND -- 2011 Holiday Book Sale with 25 Maine authors on hand to sign their books, noon-3 p.m. Friday, Nov. 25, at Portland Public Library, 5 Monument Square. For a complete list of authors, visit mainewriters.org.
ROCKLAND -- Three Midcoast authors book signing, 2-3:30 p.m. Saturday, Nov. 26, at The Reading Corner, 408 Main St. featuring "Hull Creek" author Jim Nichols of Warren, "Good Catch" author Tracy Ann Lord of Hope, and "Patch Scratching" author Steven Powell of Thomaston. For information, call 596-6651.
ROCKLAND -- As part of Rockland's Festival of Lights celebration, a book signing with local author Don Carrigan and illustrator Tom Block of "Togus: A Coon Cat Finds a Home," 1 p.m. Saturday, Dec. 3, at the Farnsworth Art Museum, 16 Museum St. Togus also will attend. For information and a complete list of holiday events, visit farnsworthmuseum.org.
THOMASTON -- George Chappell will read from his new collection of poetry, "A Fresh Footpath," 2 p.m. Saturday, Dec. 10, at The Personal Book Shop, 78 Main St. Refreshments will be served. For information, call 354-8058.
