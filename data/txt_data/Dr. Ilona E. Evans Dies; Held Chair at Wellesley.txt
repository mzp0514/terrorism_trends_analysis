Dr. Ilona E. Evans Dies; Held Chair at Wellesley

Body


Dr. Ilona Elizabeth Evans, a professor at Wellesley College and president of the American Society of International Law, died of congestive heart failure Tuesday at the Newtown-Wellesley Hospital, Wellesley, Mass. She was 63 years old and lived in Wellesley.
AN-A
Dr. Evans, the first woman to become president of the society, was elected last April. She was Elizabeth Kimball Kendall Professor of Political Science at Wellesley College at the time of her death. She had been on the faculty since 1945 and had taught international law for many years.
Dr. Evans had been a member of the American Society of International Law since 1943 and had served on the board of editors of The American Journal of International Law.
Born in Providence, R.I., she graduated from Duke University in 1940 and received her Ph.D. there in 1945. She was a member of Phi Beta Kappa and had contributed many articles to law journals. She was active in the American branch of the International Law Association, the American Association of University Women and had received awards from the Fulbright Commission, the Social Science Research Council and the American Philosophical Association.
Dr. Evans had been a Fellow at Harvard Law School several times and a visiting professor of international law at the University of Puget Sound. She was co-author and co-editor of ''Legal Aspects of International Terrorism,'' published by the American Society of International Law in 1978.
There are no immediate survivors.  A memorial service will be held at 10 A.M Saturday at St. Andrew's Episcopal Church in Wellesley.
