Women debate feminism
St. Petersburg Times (Florida)
March 15, 1992, Sunday, City Edition


Times Publishing Company
Section: FLORIDIAN; MAGAZINES; Pg. 9F
Length: 203 words
Byline: KRIS JENSEN
Body


The March 16 issue of The New Republic features Naomi Wolf (The Beauty Myth) responding to an earlier article by Camille Paglia that feminism neuters women. Ms. Wolf argues that feminism allows women to be as sexual as they want to be. Writer Karen Lehman reviews Susan Faludi's Backlash: The Undeclared War Against American Women and Gloria Steinem's Revolution From Within.
Cox News Service   POLITICS AND THE ARTS: John Frohnmayer, outgoing head of the National Endowment for the Arts, reveals in Newsweek how he became a scapegoat in presidential politics. In his first interview since announcing his resignation, he says the endowment could be doomed "unless people of good will say they won't be intimidated by this kind of cultural terrorism."
 
    HOORAY FOR HOLLYWOOD: The March 13 Entertainment Weekly takes a look at Elizabeth Taylor's career at 60. The April Architectural Digest, "Academy Awards Collector's Edition," takes readers into the homes of 35 Hollywood luminaries including Greta Garbo, Judy Garland, Henry Fonda and Cher.
 
    HOME FRONT: Good Housekeeping's April issue offers the magazine's annual home improvement guide; next month's Popular Mechanics offers 20 pages of home-maintenance tips.
