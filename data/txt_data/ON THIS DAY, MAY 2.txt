1670 The Hudson's Bay Co. was chartered by England's King Charles II.
1864 Andrew Carnegie, at 29, entered the iron-producing business by purchasing a sixth interest in the Iron City Forge Co. for $8,925. Other shareholders included Andrew Kloman and Henry Phipps.
1890 The Oklahoma Territory was organized.
1938 Ray Sprigle was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for his expose of Justice Hugo Black's affiliation with the Ku Klux Klan.
1954 Four new buildings were dedicated on the campus of the Pennsylvania College for Women, now Chatham University.
1972 A fire at the Sunshine silver mine in Kellogg, Idaho, claimed the lives of 91 workers who succumbed to carbon monoxide poisoning.
1985 J. Bruce Johnston, the chairman of the Coordinating Steel Companies Committee, said that the steel industry wound resume company-by-company bargaining in 1987 after the expiration of the present labor agreement.
2011 Osama bin Laden, the face of global terrorism and architect of the Sept. 11, 2001, attacks, was killed in a firefight with elite American forces at his Pakistan compound.
Today's birthdays: Singer Engelbert Humperdinck, 76. Actress and political activist Bianca Jagger, 67. Singer Lesley Gore, 66. Singer-songwriter Larry Gatlin, 64. Actress Christine Baranski, 60. Wrestler-turned-actor Dwayne Johnson, 40. Soccer player David Beckham, 37. Actress Ellie Kemper, 32.
Thought for today: "What experience and history teach is this: that people and governments have never learned anything from history."
-- Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel, German philosopher (1770-1831)
