The Gwinnett County Police Department has arrested 17 people it says are connected to the 59 Brim Bloods street gang.
Two of those arrested are believed to be major leaders within the gang. Wonnawon Wonzon, 29, was described by police as the gang's Atlanta leader and is now facing 31 criminal charges. Marlin Smith, 39, was described by police as the gang's leader for Virginia and North Carolina and is facing six charges.
The 59 Brim Bloods has been active in the county and metro Atlanta for about 10 years, according to the police department. The current investigation into the gang's activity in Gwinnett began in January, with detectives and officers from the Gwinnett County Police Department and deputies from the Gwinnett County Sheriff's Office.
The gang engages in a number of different criminal activities, according to the police department, including pimping, prostitution, robbery, illegal gun sales, drug trafficking, assault and murder.
In addition to the arrests, police seized 72.4 grams of fentanyl, 139 grams of heroin, 57.6 grams of cocaine, 44.6 grams of MDMA, 2.4 kilograms of meth and 18 guns. Four of the 18 guns seized were stolen, police said.
The first arrest in this investigation occurred Feb. 19,when 19-year-old Brianne Froeliger was charged with 14 offenses including theft by receiving stolen property and possession of a firearm by a convicted felon. The last arrest before the police department's Tuesday announcement was made on Oct. 28. Renaldo Lawrence, 38, was charged with multiple weapons and drug offenses.
