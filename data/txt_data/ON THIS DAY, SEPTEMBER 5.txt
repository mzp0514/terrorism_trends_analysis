1793 The Reign of Terror began during the French Revolution as the National Convention instituted harsh measures to repress counter-revolutionary activities.
1836 Sam Houston was elected president of the Republic of Texas.
1932 Organized labor in Pittsburgh held its first parade in a dozen years and ended it at West View Park, where William Green, president of the AFL, called for shorter working hours and a more equitable distribution of wealth.
1956 According to a Navy announcement, an atomic reactor for the world's first nuclear-powered surface warship would be built by Westinghouse.
1972 Terrorism struck the Munich Olympics as members of the Palestinian group Black September attacked the Israeli delegation; 11 Israelis, five guerrillas and a police officer were killed in the resulting siege.
1986 Four hijackers who had seized a Pan Am jumbo jet on the ground in Karachi, Pakistan, opened fire when the lights inside the plane failed; a total of 22 people were killed in the hijacking.
2000 The Center for Sports Medicine of the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center opened at the combined Steelers and University of Pittsburgh training facility on the South Side.
Today's birthdays: Former Federal Reserve Board chairman Paul A. Volcker, 85. Comedian-actor Bob Newhart, 83. Actress-singer Carol Lawrence, 80. Actor George Lazenby, 73. Actress Raquel Welch, 72. Singer Loudon Wainwright III, 66. Actor Michael Keaton, 61.
Thought for today: "Ideas are one thing, and what happens is another."
-- John Cage (1912-1992)
