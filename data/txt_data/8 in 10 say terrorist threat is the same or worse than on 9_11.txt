8 in 10 say terrorist threat is the same or worse than on 9/11
Charleston Gazette (West Virginia)
September 11, 2005, Sunday


Copyright 2005 Charleston Newspapers
Section: News; Pg. P11A
Length: 231 words
Byline: The Associated Press
Body


Eight in 10 people in the United States think the threat from terrorism is about the same or has grown worse than it was at the time of the attacks of Sept. 11, 2001, a poll found.
About half think the threat is at the same level and three in 10 say it has grown worse, a CBS News poll found.
Other recent findings:
s Six in 10 say they have confidence in the government's ability to protect citizens from terrorism, down from seven in 10 who felt that way before Hurricane Katrina hit. - CBS News.
s People are evenly split on whether they are satisfied with the way things are going in the war on terrorism with 51 percent satisfied, 49 percent dissatisfied. - CNN-USA Today-Gallup.
s Four in 10 say their life changed after Sept. 11 and they do not expect it will return to normal. - CNN-USA Today-Gallup.
s Some 57 percent of those surveyed say they are worried the government will not be able to provide assistance in case of a natural disaster or terrorist attack. - Time magazine.
The CBS polling was done Aug. 29-31 of 871 adults and Sept. 6-7 of 725 adults and has a margin of error of plus or minus 3 percentage points for late August and 4 percentage points for September. The CNN-USA Today-Gallup poll was conducted Aug. 28-30 and the Time poll was conducted Sept. 7-8 Both the Gallup and Time polls of about 1,000 adults have a margin of error of plus or minus 3 percentage points.
